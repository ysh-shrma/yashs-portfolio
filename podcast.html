Why I Built This

I don't have a reading habit, but I kept running into books people around me swore by. I can listen to podcasts though, so one solution was to build a tool that converts books into audio.

Except every existing tool (including NotebookLM) generates the same content for everyone, with generic examples that don't map to what I'm actually dealing with at work. A PM managing an AI agent for auto dealerships and a designer at a fintech startup should get completely different takeaways from the same book.

I built this to generate a podcast that knows who I am and what problems I'm trying to solve.

What Makes It Different

The problem with existing tools
Every book-to-audio tool gives everyone the same output: same examples, same framing, same generic takeaways regardless of who you are or what you're working on
What this does instead
You fill out a short profile (role, challenges, what you're trying to learn), and the entire podcast: framing, examples, takeaways: adapts to your context. A PM and a designer get different podcasts from the same book

How It Works

1

Extract

EPUB is parsed into clean chapter text. TOC, copyright, and blank pages are filtered out automatically.

2

Digest

Each chapter is summarised by an LLM, with your profile context injected so the digest emphasises what matters to you.

3

Script

Chapter digests are woven into a two-voice podcast script (host + author). The framing and examples are personalised to your role and challenges.

4

Synthesize

Script is converted to multi-voice audio using Edge neural voices, batched asynchronously, then stitched into a single podcast MP3.
